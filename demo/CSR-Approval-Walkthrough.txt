================================================================================
SCREEN RECORDING SCRIPT — CSR APPROVAL FLOW (HUMAN-IN-THE-LOOP)
PO Order-Fulfillment Orchestration — AI Accelerator
================================================================================

PURPOSE (read to client at the start)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
"This demo shows the human-in-the-loop path. The AI auto-resolves everything it
can confidently determine from master data, but whenever a decision carries
business risk, is ambiguous, or breaches policy, it PAUSES the pipeline and asks
a CSR to decide. This single order is engineered to trigger five different
approval gates so you can see how the agent hands off to a human, captures the
decision, and then resumes automatically."

This PO raises FIVE CSR decision gates:
   INTAKE PHASE
     1. Obsolete product — needs a substitute        (Line 1: SKU-CTG-1000)
     2. Invalid quantity                              (Line 4: qty = 0)
     3. Unit-of-measure conversion (EA -> KIT)        (Line 5: 52 EA)
     4. Unresolved buyer                              (procurement@greatlakesps.com)
   PIPELINE PHASE
     5. Pricing / margin exception                    (SKU-SHS-7700: 21.5% > 10%)


STEP 0 — SETUP (before you hit record)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Start the app and open it full screen with the chat panel visible.
- Have the PO text below ready on your clipboard.
- Start a fresh order so the demo begins clean.


STEP 1 — SUBMIT THE PURCHASE ORDER
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Action:  Click the "+" (add order) control, paste the PO text below, submit it.

Say:     "This order looks routine, but it has several problems buried in it that
         normally cost a CSR a lot of manual lookup time. Let's see how the AI
         handles them."

STEP 2 — INTAKE AGENT FLAGS THE ITEMS THAT NEED A DECISION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
On screen:  The Intake Agent extracts the PO and reviews it against master data.
            Instead of passing straight through, it reports it found several
            items that need CSR review and PAUSES. The page auto-scrolls to the
            first "CSR DECISION NEEDED" card so the CSR reads it from the top.

Say:        "The AI has already done the investigation — it checked every line
            against the catalog, lifecycle, units and the buyer directory — and
            it's surfacing only the items that genuinely need a human decision.
            It presents each one, in turn, with everything it already worked out."


STEP 3 — GATE 1: OBSOLETE PRODUCT SUBSTITUTION (Line 1)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
On screen:  A "CSR DECISION NEEDED" card for Line 1 explains SKU-CTG-1000 is
            OBSOLETE. A table shows the approved replacements with the actual vs.
            suggested SKU, prices, price difference, and a "View details" link
            for the rationale. A radio-select column lets the CSR pick one; a
            text box lets them type a different SKU instead.

Do:         Select the recommended replacement (SKU-CTG-4520), then click
            "Approve selected". (Optionally open "View details" to show the
            rationale popup first.)

Say:        "The obsolete cartridge can't be ordered as-is. The AI has already
            found the approved replacement, shown the price impact, and explained
            why it fits. I just confirm the substitution — or I could type a
            different SKU. One click and it's resolved."


STEP 4 — GATE 2: INVALID QUANTITY (Line 4)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
On screen:  A card for Line 4 flags the quantity as invalid (0). A quantity input
            appears; "Use my entry" stays disabled until a valid, positive whole
            number is entered.

Do:         Type a valid quantity (e.g. 15) and click "Use my entry".

Say:        "Line 4 came in with a zero quantity — the AI won't guess a number.
            It asks the CSR for a valid quantity, and it validates the input:
            only positive whole numbers are accepted."


STEP 5 — GATE 3: UNIT-OF-MEASURE CONVERSION (Line 5)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
On screen:  A card for Line 5 explains the customer ordered 52 EA, but this
            product is sold in KIT, where 1 KIT = 10 EA. Because 52 doesn't
            divide evenly, the AI offers two options in a table — round down to
            5 KIT (50 EA) or round up to 6 KIT (60 EA) — each with the EA
            equivalent, unit price and total price impact.

Do:         Select one option (e.g. round up to 6 KIT) and click "Approve
            selected".

Say:        "The customer ordered in each, but we sell this item in kits. The AI
            converted the units, and because it doesn't divide evenly it shows
            both rounding options with the exact price impact, so the CSR makes
            an informed call."


STEP 6 — GATE 4: UNRESOLVED BUYER
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
On screen:  A card explains the buyer email on the PO
            (procurement@greatlakesps.com) is not in the buyer directory. A table
            lists the buyers registered for this customer (name, email, role,
            cost centre) with a radio-select column; a text box allows a
            different buyer entry (with email validation).

Do:         Select the registered buyer (John Miller) and click "Approve
            selected".

Say:        "The email on the PO is a generic mailbox that isn't a registered
            buyer. The AI pulls up the authorised buyers for this account so the
            CSR can attach the order to the right person — which also drives the
            approval limits later."


STEP 7 — PIPELINE RESUMES AUTOMATICALLY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
On screen:  With all intake decisions captured, the AI resumes. The Customer
            Validation, Product Matching, and Pricing agents run in sequence.

Say:        "All four intake decisions are captured and applied to the PO, and
            the pipeline resumes on its own — the CSR doesn't restart anything."


STEP 8 — GATE 5: PRICING / MARGIN EXCEPTION (SKU-SHS-7700)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
On screen:  The Pricing & Promo Agent pauses with a yellow "CSR DECISION NEEDED
            — PRICING_EXCEPTION" banner. It explains the effective discount of
            21.5% on SKU-SHS-7700 (Digital Shower Interface System) exceeds the
            SHOWERSYS family policy limit of 10%, and shows the margin impact in
            dollars and the required approver.

Do:         Review the details and click "Approve" to override, or "Notify to
            customer" to route it.

Say:        "This is a policy gate, not a data gap. The AI built the full price
            waterfall, detected the discount breaches the margin policy, quantified
            the margin impact, and paused for approval before it lets the order
            through. The CSR approves the exception and processing continues."


STEP 9 — REMAINING LAYERS + ORDER CREATED
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
On screen:  After the pricing approval, the Credit, Inventory, Shipments and
            Approvals agents all pass, and the Order Execution Agent creates the
            order and shows the completion message with the order number and
            totals.

Approximate totals for the choices made in this walkthrough:
   Subtotal        : ~$9,583.35
   Freight         : ~$159.44
   Tax             : ~$844.83
   ORDER TOTAL     : ~$10,659.50
   (Exact totals depend on the substitute, quantity and rounding options chosen;
    the order number is generated at run time.)

Say:        "The order is created. Five decisions that would normally take a CSR
            multiple system lookups were pre-investigated by the AI, presented as
            simple one-click choices, and everything else was fully automated."


STEP 10 — SHOW THE AUDIT TRAIL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
On screen:  Scroll to the CSR Decision Audit Trail.

Say:        "Every gate is recorded — why the AI paused, what it had already
            decided automatically from master data, and the action the CSR took.
            That gives full traceability and governance over both the automated
            and the human decisions."


CLOSING TALKING POINTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- The AI does the investigation; the CSR only makes the judgement calls.
- Two kinds of gates: data reconciliation (intake) and policy breach (pipeline).
- The pipeline pauses and resumes seamlessly around each human decision.
- Guardrails are built in: quantity, email and SKU entries are all validated.
- Every decision — automated or human — is captured for audit and governance.
